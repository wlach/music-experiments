# Yamaha Keyboard / Garage band

Got my Yamaha keyboard back (I'd loaned it to a church for a while, they didn't need it so it was sitting in someone's basement... long story).

I opened up Garage Band on Mac, went through the piano tutorial again and went through first two lessons. Got irritated with Ode to Joy pretty fast
(could tell I was going down the past of "attempting to technically master music/scales that mean nothing to me") so decided to do something more interesting/exploratory.

I hooked up the keyboard to the Mac and it immediately recognized it! :tada: Selected a few samples and settled on "High Rise Pluck" as being interesting. Found a riff I liked (e3 e3 g3/2 a4/2 c4/2 b4/2) and attempted to start playing it. Sort of playful and luminous. Tried going
up and down an Octave. Ending on slightly different notes in the scale. Quite interesting.

Then tried encoding the pattern in strudel (`note("<e3@2 e3@2 g3 a4 c4 b4>*4").sound("sawtooth")`) and found it sounded _totally_ different.
Almost to the point that I though I had the scale/key wrong? But nope, double checked and even played alongside strudel. Same approximate notes,
totally different results.
It's fascinating to me how there seems to be little details of _how_ we play things that seem to make all difference in how they sound.

Must say that ChatGPT has been useful for this stage of things. It definitely gets some things wrong (e.g. it suggests syntax for strudel which outright doesn't work) but it's right about enough to give me _ideas_ of where to go. Which is all I really want at this point? I kind of expect (hope?) to throw it out later, but we'll just see.
